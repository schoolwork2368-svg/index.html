In colour theory there are colour harmonies. Colour harmonies are colors put together in a visually appealing way.

e.g. When blue and yellow are put together they pop more then blue and green would.

Hues

Colours have hues. A hue in how red issolid red compared to salmon. Is salmon more pink or red? Lighter or darker? Hues are a fancy way a saying shades. Navy blue is a shade of blue. The hue is darker.

Tones

Tones are similar to hues in that they can be lighter or darker. A lighter tone of blue will look more light gray than solid blue. A darker tone will dark gray than solid blue.

primary, secondary, and tertiary colors

A primary color is a colour that can't be broken down into more colours on the spectrum. Secondary colours are colours that are made of two primary colors. Tertiary colours are made of to or more secondary colours.

There are 3 primary colours. Red, blue, and yellow. There are three secondary colours. Orange, purple, and green. There are 9,999,994 tertiary colours. e.g. pink, violet, salmon, sky blue. If your colour has a prefix(coming before th word) it is a tertiary colour.

Shades

Shades aren't colours. They are the absence of colours. Black, white, and gray are all shades. Shades are weirder than you think. If you mix all 10M colours together you get a dark brown.
